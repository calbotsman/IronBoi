# MYO Graphics-and-Gameplay Direction

- **Author:** Zara (Art Director)
- **Date:** 2026-06-08
- **Status:** REVISE on Training Dossier direction

## Verdict: REVISE Dossier direction

The Training Dossier metaphor doesn't need to die, but it must evolve to accommodate Tosh's push for graphics and gameplay. The Dossier's core — quiet authority, typewritten labels, single coach's-ink red accent — remains intact, but we'll introduce subtle game-feel elements that elevate engagement without sacrificing restraint.

## Surfaces to receive game-feel

### 1. Active Workout
**Move:** The rep counter pulses with the beat of a metronome the user can tap to set their tempo. Completed sets fold down with a paper-fold transition, revealing the next set. This introduces rhythm and progression without gamified clutter.

### 2. Workout Tab (PlannedWorkoutDayCard)
**Move:** Day cards subtly "breathe" with a slight expansion/contraction animation when tapped, mimicking the rhythm of controlled breathing. Completed days fade to a soft gray with a paper-tear effect at the edge, signaling closure without confetti.

### 3. Exercise Sequence Player
**Move:** The kettlebell-swing sequence integrates a subtle "snap" sound effect at the apex of the swing, synced with the animation. This adds tactile feedback without breaking the minimalist aesthetic.

### 4. Onboarding
**Move:** Input fields animate with a smooth "ink flow" effect as users type, mimicking the feel of a pen on paper. This reinforces the Dossier's tactile, analog vibe while enhancing interactivity.

## Surfaces to remain untouched

- **Coach Tab:** Retain the chat bubbles on cream paper. Game-feel here would clash with the calm, conversational tone.
- **You Tab (Dossier):** Maintain typewritten labels and red accent. Introduce only subtle enhancements, like a "stamp" effect when completing a milestone (e.g., "12 Weeks Completed"), but avoid over-designing.

## Gameplay patterns to REFUSE

1. **Streak guilt:** No "Don't break your streak!" nudges or countdown timers. This undermines the calm authority of the coach.
2. **Level-up confetti:** No celebratory explosions or flashy animations. Completion is marked with restraint.
3. **Generic motivation:** No "🔥🔥🔥 You're crushing it!" messages. Feedback remains specific and actionable.
4. **Point systems:** No arbitrary points or badges. Progress is measured in tangible metrics (reps, sets, consistency).
5. **Avant-garde cosplay:** No borrowed "radical" aesthetics (e.g., neon gradients, glitch effects). Stick to the Dossier's grounded, tactile language.

## Specific revisions to Dossier direction

- **Subtle interaction:** Introduce a "stamp" effect for milestones (e.g., "12 Weeks Completed") in the You tab. This adds tactile feedback without over-designing.
- **Rhythm integration:** Use the metronome pulse and paper-fold transitions in Active Workout to reinforce the Dossier's analog feel.
- **Tactile feedback:** Add the "ink flow" effect in Onboarding to enhance interactivity while maintaining the Dossier's aesthetic.

## Final note

The Dossier metaphor survives by evolving into a hybrid of quiet authority and subtle game-feel. Graphics and gameplay enhance engagement without compromising the core identity. This approach keeps MYO Coach distinct from gym-bro apps while meeting Tosh's push for visual energy.
